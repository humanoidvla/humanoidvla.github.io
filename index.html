HAF-VLA, a hierarchical action-flow framework for humanoid VLA control.

[pdf] [arxiv] [code]
Abstract.

Humanoid robots hold great promise as general-purpose agents in human-centered environments, yet adapting generalist vision-language-action (VLA) policies to real-world humanoid loco-manipulation remains challenging. Unlike fixed-base or wheeled manipulators, humanoids must coordinate locomotion, posture, gaze, and dual-arm manipulation over a high-dimensional whole-body action space, where inaccurate base or waist motions can easily induce unstable and aggressive upper-body compensation. We propose HAF-VLA, a hierarchical action-flow framework for humanoid VLA control. Built upon a pretrained flow-matching VLA, HAF-VLA inherits the vision-language prefix encoder and action expert weights, and adapts them to humanoid whole-body control with learnable stage embeddings and cross-stage KV-cache conditioning. Instead of generating the full action chunk in a single pass, HAF-VLA progressively denoises a shared whole-body action space: first locomotion and head commands, then waist refinement, and finally full-body manipulation. To preserve structural dependencies across stages, we introduce a cross-stage KV cache conditioning mechanism, enabling subsequent action generation stages to refine and augment their outputs based on the action representations from preceding stages. Experiment results across six real-world tasks demonstrate that our framework significantly enhances the performance of existing models in humanoid loco-manipulation tasks, demonstrating an effective pathway for adapting strong mobile-manipulation VLAs to humanoid whole-body control.
