HAF-VLA, a hierarchical action-flow framework for humanoid VLA control.

[pdf] [arxiv] [code]
Abstract.

Humanoid robots face two core bottlenecks: existing vision-language-action (VLA) models cannot generate coordinated full-body motions, and offline training policies perform poorly on unseen out-of-distribution tasks. Direct online reinforcement learning on large VLA models also brings high computation and safety risks.

This work first designs a hierarchical whole-body VLA model for humanoids, which integrates visual perception, language understanding and full-body motion generation with kinematic constraints, and adopts a big-small brain structure for stable real-robot deployment. We further propose DSRL-DCT, an offline-to-online RL adaptation method. We freeze the pre-trained VLA model, reverse flow matching to map expert trajectories into latent noise space, and use DCT to compress the high-dimensional noise into low-dimensional coefficients for efficient RL optimization. The policy combines SAC reinforcement loss and BC imitation loss for stable offline pre-training and safe online fine-tuning.
